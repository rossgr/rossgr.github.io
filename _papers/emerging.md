## Abstract

Virtual Reality (VR) systems are increasingly used across consumer, commercial, and industrial settings, yet their security and privacy challenges remain underexplored. Because VR devices collect biometric and spatial data and depend on continuous connectivity, they introduce risks beyond those in
traditional computing. This paper reviews five key studies and identifies three research themes, Network
Attack Vectors, Privacy and Policy, and Physical Device Modification, while proposing a fourth, Digital
Environment Exploitation, to understand emerging threats. The analysis shows that existing research
prioritises network-based vulnerabilities, while physical and environment-level attacks remain understudied. The review highlights gaps in privacy enforcement and device weaknesses that enable user tracking
or environment infiltration, and concludes with recommendations for improved detection mechanisms.
