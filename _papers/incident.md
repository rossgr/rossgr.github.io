## Abstract

The Conti infection resulted in more than 80% of the HSE's IT systems being affected, causing cancellations in 31 of its 54  hospitals. (HSE, 2021). 700 GB of unencrypted data including protected health information (PHI) was exfiltrated (HHS, 2022).  The scale of this attack when combined with the intended target resulted in human cost as well as financial, reputational and legal repercussions; a total of 473 legal proceedings have been issued against the HSE following the aftermath of the attack (The Irish Times, 2024). This heightened pressure is often seen as a potential motivator for ransom payments from victims.
